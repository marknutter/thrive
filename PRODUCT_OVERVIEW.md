## What Are We Building?

An AI-powered go-to-market platform that lets GTM consultants (starting with you) deliver a complete outbound marketing playbook and ongoing campaign execution to clients — in hours instead of weeks, at a fraction of the cost of a traditional engagement.

The app encodes your proven GTM methodology into an AI system that can conduct a client workshop, build a playbook, coach improvements, and orchestrate outbound campaigns — with you (the consultant) guiding the process and the AI doing the heavy lifting.

**2. The Client (founder/CEO of a B2B business)**

- They can optionally pay for your continued human coaching on top

## What Does the App Actually Do?

### Phase 1: The Workshop (Building the Playbook)

**Before the meeting:**
- Client uploads whatever materials they have — sales deck, website URL, 1-pager, customer list, LinkedIn page, videos, blog posts, anything
- The AI processes all uploaded materials and identifies gaps
- The AI generates a customized question list based on what's missing

**The workshop itself (2-3 hours, voice-driven):**
- You (the consultant) and the client join a live session in the app
- The app **listens to your conversation** through separate microphones — no typing required
- It displays questions on screen, organized by section (Positioning, ICP, Personas, Market, etc.)
- As you and the client discuss, the AI extracts structured answers in real-time
- It generates smart follow-up questions based on what it hears — not just a fixed script
- If the client's answers reveal weak positioning or a too-broad ICP, the AI flags it for you
- A progress bar shows which sections are complete

**After the workshop:**
- The AI generates a complete playbook as an **editable Google Doc**, including:
  1. Positioning statement / "Our Why" and key value propositions
  2. 2-minute elevator pitch (narrative summary)
  3. 2-sentence elevator pitch (conversation starter)
  4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) descriptions
  5. Buyer and user persona definitions
- It also generates a **7-15 slide Google Slides deck** summarizing the playbook
- Both documents are editable by the client and downloadable as PDF or Microsoft Office files
- Documents include the client's branding (logo, colors) where possible

**This replaces:** The weeks-long onboarding process that a digital marketing agency would charge $20-50K for. The app gets to the same output in a single afternoon.

---

### Phase 2: Coaching & Improvements

**What happens:**
- Once the client has reviewed and finalized their Phase 1 playbook, the AI analyzes it against your frameworks and real-time market data
- It generates a prioritized list of recommendations: positioning improvements, ICP refinements, messaging updates, website copy suggestions
- These are presented in a **text-based coaching conversation** (not voice — this phase is async, the client works through it at their own pace)

**How the coaching works:**
- The AI uses a **Socratic approach** by default — asking questions to lead the client to discover the right answer themselves
- If the client is stuck or says "just tell me what to do," the AI gets specific with direct recommendations
- If the client proposes something weak (too-broad ICP, generic messaging), the AI **challenges them directly** — no sugar coating, but always with a better alternative offered
- The personality is "positive aggressive" — plays to win, pushes hard, but is respectful and motivational
- The goal: the client feels like the improvements were *their* ideas, which creates buy-in

**Output:**
- An **updated playbook** (clean replacement, not tracked changes)
- A **separate change summary** document explaining what changed, why, and the expected improved outcome for each change
- A **website recommendations document** — detailed, specific suggestions for how to update their website to reflect the new messaging and positioning (written as requirements a web developer or website builder agent could execute)
- Updated slide deck

**This replaces:** The iterative back-and-forth with a consultant over weeks/months where recommendations get watered down or lost. The AI is persistent, consistent, and doesn't forget what was agreed upon.

### Phase 3: Outbound Campaign Execution

This is where the app goes from strategy to action.

#### Campaign Planning
- The AI walks you through its **7-step campaign template** for each campaign:
  1. Campaign Goal
  2. Target Customer (from finalized ICP)
  3. Target Persona (from finalized personas)
  4. Data Needed (for personalization)
  5. Problem-Value Hypothesis (Situation → Problem → Impact → Outcome)
  6. Angle (Pain-led, Insight-led, Trigger-led, or Proof-led)
  7. Offer (what the prospect gets from engaging)
- **The AI pre-fills each step with smart defaults** based on the playbook. You review and adjust.

#### Content Creation
For each campaign, the AI generates:
- **Email sequence:** 3-touch cadence, 2 tone variations (A/B), under 100 words each
- **LinkedIn sequence:** Connection request + follow-up messages
- **Phone talking points** (if phone is in the cadence)

All content is written in the **client's voice** (the AI learns their tone and style from uploaded materials). Every piece of content is flagged for human review before anything goes out.

#### List Building (via Clay)
- You describe the signal you want to target **in plain English** (e.g., "VP of Ops at manufacturing companies with 50-200 employees who just hired a new CFO")
- The AI translates this into Clay criteria and helps you set up the signal in Clay
- Clay enriches the list and sends contacts back to the app: company name, person name, title, email, phone, LinkedIn URL
- You **review and approve the contact list** in the app before anything happens

#### Campaign Execution (via Heyreach + Email)
- Approved contacts get pushed to Heyreach (LinkedIn automation) and/or email tools
- The AI creates the campaign in the tool with the approved messaging
- **You review the campaign one final time** and hit "Launch"
- Nothing ever goes out without human approval (unless you explicitly enable "full automation" mode, which comes with warnings)

#### Signal-Driven Marketing Plays (Ongoing)
- Each month, the AI works with you to evaluate current signal-driven plays and suggest improvements or new ones based on trends
- Example plays: "New CTO hired at ICP companies," "Series A funding in our target vertical," "Companies posting job listings for roles our product replaces"
- Each signal generates a fresh contact list + tailored campaign
- You review and approve before each run

#### Campaign Monitoring
- **Dashboard** showing real-time campaign status: sent, opened, replied, meetings booked
- **Auto-pause** for underperforming campaigns (low open rates, spam complaints) with AI diagnosis of what went wrong
- **Weekly scorecard** generated every Monday: metrics per campaign, AI analysis of what worked, specific recommendations for the coming week

**Default guardrails (overridable):**
- Lists under 500 accounts (ideally ~300)
- Max 2 contacts per company at the same time
- Cadences always include LinkedIn + phone + email (not email-only)
- Emails under 100 words
- 2 tone variations per email

**This replaces:** A marketing coordinator or BDR team running outbound manually. The AI handles the list building, content writing, tool setup, and monitoring. The human makes the strategic calls and approves before execution.

## How the AI Thinks

The app isn't a generic chatbot — it's trained on your specific GTM methodology:

- **Your Master Checklist** (all 8 sections, A through H) drives the workshop structure
- **Your campaign planning template** structures every outbound campaign
- **The Challenger method** is used when the client's product is new or not well-understood by buyers
- **MEDDIC** is used when the product is in a well-known category and the sale is more transactional
- **The AI auto-detects which methodology fits** based on the client's business and market

The AI also supplements your frameworks with real-time external research — competitor analysis, market trends, industry benchmarks — so recommendations aren't limited to what's in your documents alone.

## What the App Does NOT Do (V1)

- **Sales process coaching** (Stages 0-6, deal strategy, demo prep) — future phase
- **Content production** for podcasts, videos, or web events — humans handle this
- **Spreadsheet outputs** (weekly scorecards as spreadsheets, deal forecasters, lead tracking sheets) — future phase
- **Actually build/modify the client's website** — generates detailed recommendations documents instead
- **Run campaigns without human approval** — unless the user explicitly enables full-automation mode
